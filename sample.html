Personalized Career Plan for Diversity Specialist


Diversity Specialist

Description of the Role

The Diversity Specialist is organizing and implementing all diversity training programs within the organization. S/he will assist management with not only understanding individual differences but also to work with and manage others in a manner that maximizes everyone’s contribution to the organization. In addition they will create an environment where not only are diverse people recruited into the organization but also both the company and the individual work together to develop a mutually beneficially relationship.

Preparation for the Role

The following experience and recommended qualifications have been cited as useful for preparation as a Diversity Specialist. Those seeking this position should have proven experience and/or qualifications in one or more of the following areas:

Key Accountabilities

   ▪ Assist with the development of policies and programs to attract, retain and promote a diverse workforce for the
     organization
   ▪ Determine the appropriateness of introducing diversity initiatives and consider the unique needs of the
     organization
   ▪ Train and educate employees and mangers on how to recognize, accommodate and appreciate individual
     differences
   ▪ Develop, write, and coordinate diversity training manuals and materials
   ▪ Develop a means of measuring the effectiveness of the diversity initiative
   ▪ Keep current on diversity programs and development
   ▪ Provide coaching and diversity skills building to all levels of the organization
   ▪ Determine the appropriateness of introducing diversity initiatives and consider the unique needs of the
     organization
   ▪ Acts as a liaison within the company to ensure coordination in the implementation of the diversity strategy
     throughout the business
   ▪ Generates and analyzes diversity metrics

Primary Interfaces

This role requires the Organizational Development Specialist and Trainer to interact with a variety of people and departments, which may include:

   ▪ Internal Staff to determine diversity needs
   ▪ Diversity Affinity Groups (where applicable) to discuss the needs of diverse employee groups
   ▪ Organizational Development Consultant for direction, feedback and guidance
   ▪ Third Party Vendor to seek outside help with special diversity training

Challenges in the Role

Typical challenges associated with the Diversity Specialist may include:

   ▪ Dealing with and overcoming employee resistance to participating in diversity initiatives
   ▪ Handling employees that are discriminatory
   ▪ Continually identifying barriers to the success of diversity
